// The story

Built out of frustration

After years working across genomics, proteomics, and drug discovery pipelines — at institutions ranging from KAUST to leading genomics labs — I kept hitting the same walls. Tools that worked on one cluster broke on another. Pipelines that a postdoc built couldn't be reproduced six months later. AI assistants that couldn't speak bioinformatics.

"Every lab was solving the same problems in isolation. I decided to build the platform I always wished existed."

OmniBioAI started as a personal toolkit and grew into a full production platform — 11 microservices, 50+ pipelines, agentic AI reasoning, and clinical-grade provenance tracking — all in a single desktop install.

The mission

Modern bioinformatics workflows are fragmented, slow, and difficult to reproduce across environments. A graduate student's analysis shouldn't break when they move to a new HPC. A clinical team shouldn't have to revalidate pipelines every time a tool version changes.

OmniBioAI is my answer: a reproducible, AI-native platform that runs identically on a laptop, a Slurm cluster, or AWS — with full provenance from raw FASTQ to final biological insight.

The platform is in private beta, actively accepting researchers who want to accelerate their multi-omics work with production-grade infrastructure and AI-powered analysis.
